Executive Summary

The Business Case at a Glance

A leading pharmaceutical manufacturer sought a structured, evidence-based intervention to address recurring behavioural challenges. Not a generic soft-skills workshop, but a diagnostically-grounded transformation programme.

Business Challenge

  • Confusion between responsibility and accountability across all levels
  • Resistance to feedback and peer observation
  • Gossip, blame-shifting, and low psychological safety
  • Ethical ambiguity and inconsistent professional standards

Diagnostic Approach

  • Structured stakeholder interviews across functions
  • Behavioural observation mapped against competency framework
  • Gap analysis using Third Order's TNI model
  • Learning design built directly from the diagnostic findings

Key Findings

  • Accountability was the biggest gap by far
  • People had very different ideas of what professionalism meant
  • How people communicated created friction that did not need to be there
  • Adaptability and openness to feedback both needed work

Qualitative Outcomes

  • Participants took more ownership and communicated more proactively
  • People were more open to feedback than before the programme
  • Managers noticed better conduct and professional presence in their teams
  • The culture felt different after the programme wrapped up
The Business Challenge

What Was Happening on the Ground

Eight distinct behavioural patterns were consistently observed, each pointing to a competency gap, not a lack of intent.

⚖️

Responsibility vs Accountability

People completed tasks but did not own the outcomes. The work got done. The accountability did not.

🔄

Resistance to Feedback

Feedback landed as criticism. Defensive reactions were becoming the norm.

💬

Gossip & Unproductive Talk

Side conversations replaced direct ones, and trust took the hit.

🤝

Weak Professional Boundaries

No one agreed on what professional conduct actually looked like, so standards varied widely.

👂

Low Respect for Viewpoints

Differing viewpoints got ignored, and teams stopped working across boundaries.

🧭

Ethical & Integrity Concerns

Ethics were applied unevenly, which is a real problem in a regulated environment.

⚡

Low Initiative & Adaptability

Participants needed direction before acting. Proactive responses to change were rare.

🎯

Culture Misalignment

Collective norms did not match stated organisational values, so what people experienced day-to-day diverged from what leadership described.

The organisation did not want a generic soft-skills session. They wanted a structured, diagnostic-led intervention that would surface root behavioural causes, and address them with precision.

Competency Architecture

Four Pillars of Behavioural Change

The intervention was structured around four core competencies, each defined, grounded in behavioural indicators, and connected to business relevance.

01

Accountability & Ownership

Taking responsibility for outcomes, not just completing tasks, regardless of external factors.

Proactively communicates risks and setbacks without prompting
Does not deflect blame; frames challenges as problems to solve
Follows through on commitments and tracks own progress
Escalates with context, not excuses
02

Professionalism

Consistent demonstration of workplace standards in conduct, language, presentation, and ethical behaviour.

Maintains composure and presence in all settings
Avoids gossip; redirects unproductive conversations
Upholds integrity even when unobserved
Demonstrates punctuality, reliability, and follow-through
03

Communication

Expressing ideas clearly, asserting boundaries respectfully, and navigating difficult conversations with purpose.

Uses HEAT framework in difficult conversations
Practises the 3 V's of communication (verbal, vocal, visual)
Asserts opinions without aggression or passive withdrawal
Actively listens and acknowledges others' viewpoints
04

Adaptability & Initiative

Navigating uncertainty, embracing change, responding to feedback as growth, and taking initiative without direction.

Moves through Change Curve: explores before committing
Views feedback as data, not judgment
Demonstrates proactivity in ambiguous situations
Self-initiates learning and improvement
Deep Dive: Accountability & Ownership

The Accountability Ladder

Accountability was identified as the single highest-priority gap. The Accountability Ladder showed participants where they stood, and what moving upward required in practice.

Moving Up
Rung 6, Make It Happen

Full ownership. Creates solutions and drives outcomes.

Rung 5, Find Solutions

Identifies options and recommends the path forward.

Rung 4, Own the Problem

Acknowledges ownership and commits to resolving it.

Rung 3, Acknowledge Reality

Accepts a problem exists and stops minimising it.

Rung 2, Wait & Hope

Aware of issues but waits passively for someone else to act.

Rung 1, Blame & Excuses

Deflects and attributes failure externally.

Why Accountability Was the Priority

In a pharmaceutical manufacturing environment, tasks completed without outcomes owned creates compliance risk, quality gaps, and cultural breakdown. The gap between "doing the job" and "owning the result" was the most consequential finding.

Desired Future Behaviour

Participants were supported to move from Rung 1–2 (blame/wait) to Rung 4–6 (own / solve / make happen), through structured reflection, caselet-based practice, and manager-reinforced accountability rituals.

Intervention Approach

Each cohort explored a pharma-specific caselet where someone completes a task (responsibility) but doesn't ensure results (accountability). The Ladder was used as the debrief framework, allowing teams to self-diagnose and commit to movement.

Stakeholder Recommendations

Sustaining What Was Started

Behaviour change is not sustained by a workshop alone. These recommendations embed learning into the systems, rhythms, and culture of the organisation.

🎯

Manager Reinforcement Rituals

Use the 30-day check-in protocol as a recurring practice. Quarterly behavioural conversations should be normalised across all levels.

📊

Behaviour Tracking

A simple qualitative tracker to monitor commitment cards and self-reported progress, giving HR visibility without over-engineering.

💡

Coaching Integration

Targeted coaching for managers needing support in reinforcing behavioural norms, especially in accountability conversations.

📚

Continuous Learning Nudges

Fortnightly micro-learning nudges: short reflections, scenarios, and articles sent between formal sessions.

🌐

Culture Initiative Alignment

Connect the competency framework to performance reviews, onboarding, and values-alignment so the language sticks across the organisation.
